Figure 1 shows back calculations

From figure 1 we can see that shear stress remains constant throughout the beam. The bending stress at fixed support is not a reliable result due to the stress singularity therefore the bending stress at 100 mm is what is used to compare the results

Figure 2

Figure 2 shows shear and bending stress distribution on the beam near the fixed support

Figure 3 shows 3D convergence test ANSYS data

Figure 4 shows 3D convergence test

Figure 3 and 4 shows shear and bending stress near the foxed support of the beam. From iteration to iteration we decreased the mesh size. From the graph we can see that the shear and bending stress are increasing exponentially iteration to iteration. This is because we are taking the stress at a fixed point. And due to the stress singularity our bending and shear stress diverges instead of converging.

Stresses 100 mm away from fixed support

Figure 5

Figure 5 shows shear and bending stress distribution on the beam 100 mm away from the fixed support

Figure 6 shows 3D convergence test ANSYS data

Figure 7 shows 3D convergence test

Figure 6 and 7 shows shear and bending stress 100 mm away from the foxed support. From the graph we can see that at 100 mm away bending stress converges to a value of around 54 MPa which is what was calculated in backhand calculations. This is because at 100 mm away the stress singularity caused from fixed support has no effect on the stresses. And the shear also converges to a value of around 1.5 MPa.
